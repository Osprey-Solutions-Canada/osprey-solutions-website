The 10 Elements of a Perfectly Optimized GBP

1. Business Name, Address & Phone (NAP Consistency)

Your business name must match exactly across every platform — Google, Yelp, Facebook, BBB, your website. One inconsistency (like "LLC" on Google but not on Yelp) can hurt your ranking.

What we do: Audit all citations across 50+ directories and fix every inconsistency.

2. Primary & Secondary Categories

Your primary category is the single most important ranking factor in local search. Choose wrong and you won't show up for your core services.

  • Roofer → Primary: "Roofing Contractor"
  • General Contractor → Primary: "General Contractor," Secondary: "Home Builder," "Remodeling Contractor"
  • HVAC → Primary: "HVAC Contractor," Secondary: "Air Conditioning Contractor," "Heating Contractor"

What we do: Research which categories your top-ranking competitors use and match or beat them.

3. Business Description

You get 750 characters. Most contractors waste them with generic copy like "We provide quality service at affordable prices." That tells Google nothing.

What works: Specific services, specific areas, specific credentials.

Bad: "ABC Roofing is a family-owned business providing quality roofing services."

Good: "ABC Roofing installs and repairs asphalt shingle, metal, and flat roofs across Dallas, Fort Worth, and Arlington TX. GAF Master Elite certified. 15 years in business. Emergency leak repair available 24/7. Licensed, bonded, insured."

4. Services & Products Section

Most contractors leave this blank. It's a massive missed opportunity. Google reads this section to understand what you offer and match you to searches.

What we do: Add every service with a detailed description and link to the corresponding page on your website.

5. Photos & Videos

Businesses with 100+ photos get 520% more calls than those with fewer than 10. Not stock photos — real photos of your work.

What to post:

  • Before/after project photos
  • Team photos (builds trust)
  • Truck/van photos (shows legitimacy)
  • Completed job photos with location context
  • Short videos of work in progress

What we do: Add 5-10 optimized, geotagged photos per month for each client.

6. Google Business Posts

Think of these like social media posts that appear directly on your Google listing. Most contractors never use them.

Post types that work:

  • Project completions with before/after
  • Seasonal offers (storm damage repair, winter prep)
  • Community involvement
  • Tips and educational content

What we do: Publish 2-4 posts per month with keyword-rich descriptions and compelling images.

7. Reviews (The #1 Trust Signal)

Reviews are the single biggest factor in whether someone calls you or your competitor.

The numbers:

  • 200 reviews at 4.5 stars beats 5 reviews at 5.0 stars every time
  • Businesses that respond to every review rank higher in local search
  • Reviews mentioning specific services help you rank for those services

What we do: Set up automated review request sequences, respond to every review with keyword-rich responses, and flag negative reviews for immediate attention.

8. Q&A Section

The Q&A section on your GBP is searchable by Google. Most contractors ignore it, leaving customers to ask questions that go unanswered.

What we do: Pre-populate the Q&A with 10-15 common questions and detailed answers — effectively creating a mini FAQ that Google indexes.

9. Attributes & Highlights

Google lets you add attributes like "Women-owned," "Veteran-owned," "Licensed," "Insured," and service-specific options. These appear as badges on your profile and help with filtering.

What we do: Enable every relevant attribute for your business.

10. Insights & Performance Tracking

Google provides data on how people find you, what actions they take, and which photos perform best. Most contractors never look at this.

What we do: Review insights monthly, identify trends, and adjust strategy based on real data — not guesswork.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does Google Business Profile management cost?
Professional GBP management for contractors typically costs between $150-300/month depending on the scope. At Osprey Solutions, our GBP management packages include photo uploads, post creation, review management, and monthly reporting.
Can I manage my own Google Business Profile?
Absolutely. This guide covers everything you need. But most contractors tell us they started managing it themselves, did it for two weeks, then got busy with actual contracting work. Consistency is what drives results — and that's hard to maintain when you're running a business.
How long does it take to see results from GBP optimization?
Most contractors see measurable improvements within 30-60 days — more profile views, more calls, more direction requests. Ranking in the top 3 map pack for competitive terms can take 3-6 months depending on your market.
Do I need a physical office to have a Google Business Profile?
No. Service-area businesses (like most contractors) can have a GBP without showing a physical address. You set your service area by city or radius, and Google shows you for relevant searches in those areas.
What's the difference between GBP and Google Ads?
Your Google Business Profile is free and appears in the map pack based on relevance, distance, and prominence. Google Ads are paid advertisements that appear at the top of search results. Both work — but your GBP is the foundation. Without a strong profile, even paid ads underperform.
