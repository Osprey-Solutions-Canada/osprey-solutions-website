The Contractor's Guide to Getting Found by AI Search

Homeowners are asking ChatGPT and Perplexity to find contractors. If your business isn't structured for AI search, you're invisible. Here's how to fix it.

By Osprey Solutions·March 27, 2026·7 min read

A New Kind of Search Is Here

Homeowners are no longer just Googling "roofer near me." They're asking AI:

  • "ChatGPT, who's the best roofer in Dallas with good reviews?"
  • "Perplexity, how much does roof replacement cost in Texas?"
  • "Gemini, find me a licensed contractor for kitchen remodeling in Austin"

When AI answers these questions, it pulls from websites, reviews, and structured content across the internet. If your business isn't showing up in these AI-generated answers, you're missing an entirely new lead channel.

This isn't replacing Google. It's adding another layer on top of it. And the contractors who optimize for it now will have a massive head start.

How AI Search Finds Contractors

AI models like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini pull from three main sources:

1. Search Indexes

  • ChatGPT uses Bing's index (not Google's)
  • Perplexity has its own crawler
  • Gemini uses Google's index

Takeaway: You need to be indexed and ranking on both Google AND Bing

2. High-Trust Databases

  • Google Business Profile (reviews, ratings, business info)
  • Yelp, BBB, Angi, HomeAdvisor
  • Industry directories

Takeaway: Your presence on trusted third-party platforms matters more than ever

3. Website Content

  • Clear, structured answers to common questions
  • FAQ sections with specific, complete answers
  • Service pages with detailed descriptions
  • Blog posts that demonstrate expertise

Takeaway: Your website content needs to be structured for machines to read and cite

7 Steps to Optimize for AI Search

1. Claim and Optimize Everything

AI cross-references multiple sources. Be present and consistent on Google Business Profile, Bing Places, Yelp, BBB, Angi, Facebook Business Page, and industry-specific directories.

2. Structure Your Content for Direct Answers

AI models look for clear, concise answers they can cite. Structure your content so subheadings are phrased as questions and the first 1-2 sentences under each heading directly answer the question.

3. Build FAQ Sections on Every Service Page

FAQ sections are gold for AI search. Each Q&A pair is a potential citation. Write 5-10 FAQs per service page and use schema markup (FAQPage JSON-LD).

4. Earn Reviews That Mention Services and Locations

AI reads reviews. A review that says "Best roof replacement company in Dallas" tells AI that you're relevant for that service in that location.

5. Add Author and Business Credentials

AI evaluates E-E-A-T signals. Make your expertise obvious with author bios, "About Us" pages with team qualifications, license numbers, insurance details, and association memberships.

6. Get Cited by Other Sources

AI trusts content that other trustworthy sources link to. Build citations through guest posts, local news mentions, manufacturer partner pages, and Chamber of Commerce listings.

7. Submit to Bing

Most contractors optimize for Google and completely ignore Bing. ChatGPT uses Bing's index. Submit your sitemap to Bing Webmaster Tools — it takes 5 minutes and opens an entire AI search channel.

The Opportunity Is Right Now

Most contractors haven't even heard of AI search optimization. That means competition is near zero. The businesses that start optimizing for AI search now will be the ones AI models learn to recommend — and it's much harder to unseat an incumbent once AI has "learned" to trust them.

This is a 6-12 month head start over your competitors. Don't waste it.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a separate strategy for AI search?
Not entirely. Most AI search optimization overlaps with good SEO practices — structured content, strong reviews, consistent NAP, and E-E-A-T signals. The main additions are: structuring content for direct answers, building FAQ sections with schema, and ensuring you're indexed on Bing.
Is AI search replacing Google?
No. AI search is adding a new layer on top of traditional search. Google still drives the majority of local contractor leads. But AI search is growing rapidly, and contractors who appear in AI results get a significant competitive advantage.
How do I know if I'm showing up in AI search?
Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Gemini to recommend contractors in your service area. If you're not mentioned, you need to optimize. If you are — great, now work on being recommended first.
