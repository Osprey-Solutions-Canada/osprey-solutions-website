5 Google Business Profile Mistakes Costing You Leads

By Osprey Solutions|March 2026

Your GBP Isn't Just "Not Helping" — It's Hurting You

Most contractors think their Google Business Profile is fine. They set it up, added a few photos, and forgot about it. But a neglected GBP doesn't just fail to attract customers — it actively pushes them to competitors who look more legitimate.

We manage 83 contractor GBPs. Here are the 5 problems we see in nearly every new account.

1. Your Last Photo Was Uploaded Over 6 Months Ago

Google tracks how recently your profile was updated. Businesses that add photos regularly get 520% more calls than those that don't.

When a homeowner sees your competitor's profile with fresh project photos from last week, and yours has the same three blurry photos from 2023 — who do they call?

Fix it: Upload 5-10 real photos every month. Before/after project shots, team photos, and truck photos. Geotagging helps.

2. You Have Fewer Than 50 Reviews

Reviews are the #1 factor homeowners use to choose between contractors. Volume matters more than perfect scores.

The data: 200 reviews at 4.5 stars converts better than 10 reviews at 5.0 stars. People don't trust perfection — they trust volume. Fix it: Ask every customer for a review within 24 hours of job completion. Text them a direct link. Make it one click. Do this for 6 months and watch your lead volume transform.

3. You Haven't Posted in Over a Month

Google Business Posts are like social media posts that appear directly on your listing. Most contractors never use them. That signals to both Google and customers that your business isn't active.

Fix it: Post 2-4 times per month. Share completed projects, seasonal offers, and tips. Each post should include a keyword-rich description and a quality image.

4. You're Not Responding to Reviews

Not responding to reviews — even positive ones — is a missed opportunity. Google sees review responses as an engagement signal. Customers see it as proof you care.

Fix it: Respond to every review within 24 hours. Thank positive reviewers by name and mention the specific service. For negative reviews, respond professionally and take the conversation offline. Pro tip: Include keywords naturally in your responses. "Thanks for choosing us for your roof replacement in Dallas, John!" sends ranking signals to Google.

5. Your Services Section Is Empty

The Services section tells Google exactly what you offer. If it's blank, Google has to guess — and it guesses wrong.

Fix it: Add every service with a detailed description. "Roof Repair" becomes "Emergency and scheduled roof repair for asphalt shingle, metal, and flat roofs. We handle leak detection, storm damage repair, flashing replacement, and insurance claim assistance across [service area]."

The Quick Audit

Check your own GBP right now:

  • [ ] Photos uploaded in the last 30 days?
  • [ ] 50+ reviews?
  • [ ] Google Post published in the last 2 weeks?
  • [ ] Every review has a response?
  • [ ] Services section fully populated?

If you checked fewer than 3 boxes, your GBP is actively losing you leads.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I update my Google Business Profile?

Weekly at minimum. Add photos and respond to reviews weekly. Post at least twice per month. Update your services section whenever you add or change offerings.

Can a bad Google Business Profile hurt my Google Ads performance?

Yes. Google Ads quality scores are influenced by your landing page experience, which includes your GBP for local searches. A weak profile means higher ad costs and lower ad positions.

How long does it take to fix a neglected GBP?

You can implement all five fixes in a single afternoon. The impact on rankings and calls typically shows within 30-60 days of consistent management.
